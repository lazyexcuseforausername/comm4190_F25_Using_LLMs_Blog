To effectively play the game of 20 Questions, follow this structured approach:

  1. Initial Question: Begin by asking a broad question to gather initial information. For example:

    • “What is your favorite color?”

  2. First Answer: Depending on their response (yes or no), proceed accordingly.

  3. Exploring Categories:

    • If the answer was yes, ask specific questions about that category to explore further, such as:
      • “Does your favorite color come in shades of red and pink?”
      • “What are the common shades associated with your favorite color?”

  4. Exploring Attributes:

    • After narrowing down to a subset of possibilities, ask more specific yes-or-no questions related to attributes within that category.

  5. Adjusting Based on Answers: Continue asking questions based on their answers until you can identify the object.

  6. Final Guess: Once all relevant questions are asked, make your final guess confidently.

Example Sequence: - Q1: “What is your favorite color?” Answer: Yes
- Then ask: “Does your favorite color come in shades of red and pink?”

This method allows for both broad exploration and detailed questioning to identify the object.
